Session 9

Aquarius 23rd, 159 continued

Delekhan and Kyruss examined the remains of the clockwork huntsman and hounds. They concluded that they were arcane clockworks. Unusually, however, the huntsman seemed to possess a mind and wit of his own. The Connaughtons, shocked to still be alive and in their home, hosted the Heirs for the evening.

Aquarius 24th

Cold, light winds, light snow. Kyruss cooked a hearty breakfast from the Connaughtons' stored oats and salted meat. Outside, Bridgette examined the heavy tracks the clockworks had made. Despite the wind and snow, she was able to follow them, with the Heirs in tow, for sixteen miles, where they spotted a tall wooden watchtower. It clearly wasn't built for flesh and blood beings, as it had open walls and no provisions of any kind. From its upper deck, Delekhan's keen elven eyes spotted a small town in the distance. The Heirs made camp below the watchtower.

Aquarius 25th

Warmer. The Heirs walked for the day toward the town, camping in the slushy grasslands.

Aquarius 26th.

Rain. The Heirs reached the hamlet of Torbay, population 100. Delekhan sent his owl familiar to scout. They saw a half-orc smith working his forge, and a few others about town, but each fled at the arrival of the travelers. The Heirs approached the smithy's shop, and talked their way inside. Gradually, they wore down the smith, Elihu, until he conversed with them. Elihu had never met another half-orc, and had wondered if he was the only one of his kind. He listed the rules, which he knew well, though he couldn't recall how:

#1 - no religious practices - the most important rule
#2 - no travel unless you can reach your destination and return home before the sun sets
#3 - no written words
#4 - no speaking to travelers
#5 - no symbols, drawings, or map-making
#6 - no magic

The Heirs assured Elihu that they'd defeated a huntsman, and the rules were over. However, their conversation broke rule #5, and a huntsman approached them. The Heirs evaded capture and tore it apart, primarily at the hands of Lanaster, supercharged by Kyruss' magic and his own smite. Afterward, Kogrash and Elihu worked the forge, and Kogrash observed that Elihu's techniques were centuries out of date. The Heirs asked Elihu more questions, learning that a year before, a farmer who'd said a prayer of thanks had been captured by the huntsman, who lived in a small shed in Torbay, and dragged away to the northwest.

Aquarius 27th

Heavy rain. The Heirs said their goodbyes, and headed northwest. They stopped at the largest farm in the area, McKenzie farm, but the farmers refused to converse.

Aquarius 28th

Light wind. The Heirs ran into mountains, and diverted north.

Aquarius 29th

Light rain. Kogrash spotted a switchback path up the mountain to a plateau. After an eight hour climb, the Heirs spotted a great rectangular building with a protruding tower, which was exceptionally well-maintained. Delekhan and Kogrash opened the double doors, seeing four clockwork soldiers and two clockwork servants, who were scrubbing the walls.

The soldiers called out that there was an intrusion, and to follow plan alpha. Eight more servants appeared, and ran for the staircase in the middle of the hall, while the soldiers closed in to do battle. Seizing the opportunity of a lifetime, Damemon cast a fireball, which annihilated six servants and crippled two soldiers. The soldiers fought on, delaying the Heirs while two servants made it up the stairs.

Keeping their momentum going, the Heirs headed upstairs, where Kogrash was confronted by a barricade of wooden desks.
